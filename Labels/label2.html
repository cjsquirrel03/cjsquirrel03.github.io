Museum of Percussion Instruments

Flexatone, Playertone Company of New York, 1922

The flexatone is a percussion instrument known for its eerie, wavering sound. It consists of a thin sheet of flexible metal attached to a wire frame with two wooden handles. Small wooden or metal balls mounted on flexible rods strike the metal sheet as the player shakes the instrument. By bending the metal sheet with thumb pressure, the performer alters the pitch, creating a distinctive glissando effect. Popularized in vaudeville, early film scores, and 20th-century orchestral works, the flexatone is often used to evoke dreamlike, comic, or otherworldly atmospheres. Though simple in construction, it produces a surprisingly expressive range of sounds.
