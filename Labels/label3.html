Museum of Percussion Instruments

Chimes or Tubular Bells, Paris, c.1860

Tubular bells, also known as orchestral chimes, are a set of long metal tubes suspended from a frame and struck with a mallet. Each tube is carefully tuned to a specific pitch, allowing the instrument to produce clear, bell-like tones. Unlike church bells, which are cast and fixed in place, tubular bells are designed for concert performance and portability. Introduced into orchestras in the late 1800s, tubular bells are often used to evoke ceremonial, sacred, or dramatic moments. Their ringing, sustained sound can cut through a full ensemble, adding brilliance and grandeur to symphonic and film music alike.
